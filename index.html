Common Questions

Answers, before you ask

The questions we hear most often from people shopping for a plan.

When can I enroll in a health plan?
ACA Open Enrollment typically runs from November 1 through January 15 each year. Outside that window, you can still enroll if you have a "Qualifying Life Event" such as losing other coverage, moving, getting married, having a baby, or certain income changes. Tell us your situation and we'll let you know your options.
How much will I pay? Do I qualify for a subsidy?
Most people who buy individual coverage qualify for premium tax credits (subsidies) that can substantially lower their monthly cost. Eligibility depends on household size, income, and where you live, not just a single income threshold. We'll calculate it for you for free.
Will my doctor or prescription be covered?
Networks and drug formularies vary by plan. When we compare plans for you, we look at the specific doctors and medications you want covered so you don't end up with a "cheap" plan that doesn't include them.
What about pre-existing conditions?
Under the Affordable Care Act, ACA-compliant plans cannot deny you coverage or charge you more because of a pre-existing condition. That includes diabetes, cancer, pregnancy, mental health conditions, and everything in between.
How do you get paid? Is there really no fee?
We're compensated by insurance carriers via a standard commission when you enroll in a plan through us. You pay the same premium whether you enroll through Camino Health, directly with the carrier, or through HealthCare.gov. There's no separate fee for our help.
Can I change plans later if I'm not happy?
You can switch plans during the next Open Enrollment Period, or sooner if you have a Qualifying Life Event. We stay available year-round, so if your situation changes, just call us.
Is Camino Health a government website?
No. Camino Health is a private, independent insurance brokerage (a DBA of Auxa Holdings LLC). We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to the federal Health Insurance Marketplace, Medicare, Medicaid, or any government agency. For the official federal marketplace, visit HealthCare.gov.
